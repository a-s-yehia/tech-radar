How to read this radar

Each dot is a technology, placed by how it scored across the rubric. Position is meaningful in three ways:

Distance from center Driven by the weaker of a technology's readiness and performance scores — specifically distance = 1 − min(readiness, performance), using normalized 0–1 scores. A technology only sits near the center if it scores well on both; a low score on either pushes it outward. So closer = more ready and proven on every front.
Angle within a quadrant Each quadrant has a base angle; a dot is then nudged by (performance − readiness) × 30°. Equal scores sit at the quadrant's center line; a tilt toward the readiness or performance axis means that dimension scored higher.
Dot size Larger dots have higher impact — financial savings, mission alignment, return on investment, and future-proofing.
Quadrant Which application area the technology belongs to: Inspection, Construction, Maintenance, or Design.

The rings — adoption timeline

Adopt — ready for deployment Trial — promising; pilot projects Assess — explore and monitor Hold — not recommended yet
